ANALYSIS OF PAH IN SPENT COOKING OIL

GC-MSD


Introduction

Vegetable oils are an important food source and cooking medium all over the world. Polynuclear aromatic hydrocarbons are lipophilic compounds which are produced as a byproduct of the combustion of organic matter. Some PAHs have been confirmed to be carcinogenic.


Samples

A spent cooking oil sample derived from fish frying was obtained from a local food vendor.


Extraction Procedure

  • 1 g of the oil sample was transferred into a 50 ml centrifuge tube.
  • 5 ml of acetonitrile was added and shaken vigorously for 1 min.
  • Centrifuge for 3 min at 3000 rpm.
  • An aliquot (3 ml) of the acetonitrile phase (upper layer) was transferred into a 15 ml screw capped vial.
  • Vial containing the extract was placed in the freezer for 1.5 hours.
  • Frozen lipids were filtered through a clean cotton wool into a 15 ml screw capped centrifuge tube containing PSA (25 mg /ml extract).
  • The tube was shaken vigorously for 30 sec and centrifuged for 3 min at 3000 rpm
  • An aliquot of the cleaned-up extract was transferred into a screw capped GC vial.

Calibration

PAHs standard, 2000ppm (Catalog Number: H-QME-01) containing 24 environmental PAHs components was purchased from AccuStandard.

Four (5) point serial dilution calibration standards (25, 50,100,250,500ppb) was prepared from the stock and used to calibrate the GC-MS.
